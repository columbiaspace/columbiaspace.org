Columbia’s RASC-AL mission is a team of dedicated engineers and scientists seeking to expand the boundaries of lunar and martian exploration. In the past, challenges have been based around the development of lunar satellites and martian rovers with potential implications for future Artemis missions. Using hands-on involvement and technical proposal writing skills, we spend the year developing and fine-tuning innovative technology that takes humans to the Moon and Mars and ensures that we can thrive there. If selected, the team will build a prototype to present and demonstrate its capabilities at the annual RASC-AL Forum to NASA and NIA personnel in June.

This year’s objective is to design a mission concept aimed at reducing the risks associated with human exploration/presence on Mars and helping to foster a permanent human presence there. The team will have total freedom to develop a start-to-finish mission plan that showcases an innovative approach to a martian exploration.
